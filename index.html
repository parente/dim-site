Descent Into Madness

An audio horror adventure game, reborn on the web

Play Now

What is DIM?

Descent Into Madness is a audio adventure game involving exploration, puzzle-solving, and, occassionally, fast reflexes. Why did you awaken strapped to an operating table? Who is the doctor speaking in the laboratory recording you just found? And exactly what are those strange sounds you hear coming from the basement?

Can I play?

If you can use the four arrow keys, read or listen to English, and tolerate B-movie horror, you can play this game. If you're using a recent version of Safari or Chrome, your browser will even permit it. Jump in and play if you're ready, or check out the help if you're scared ... er, not.

Help, I'm stuck!

If you get stuck, have a look at the help section. It explains the basic controls and even offers some tips for the more challenging parts of the game. If all else fails, contact me.

What about ...

Have a suggestion about how to improve the game? Thought of an idea for a new game? Got a knack for voice acting, creating sound effects, drawing scene stills, or composing music? Want to donate your time as a developer? Wonderful! See how you can get involved in creating game content today.

Who built this?

In 2005, two then-UNC undergraduates, Eden Kung and Matt Clark, created the original DIM game on Windows XP. It became a cult classic of sorts among kids at the NC Governor Morehead School.

In 2012, I set out to bring DIM to the web with two goals. First, I wanted to reinvigorate this entertaining audio game for a new generation of kids to enjoy. And second, I wanted to build a "choose your own adventure" game engine for future endeavors.
